Virus

High Severity

What is a Virus?

A computer virus is a type of malicious software program ("malware") that, when executed, replicates by inserting copies of itself into other computer programs, data files, or the boot sector of the hard drive. Viruses are often spread through infected email attachments, downloads from untrustworthy websites, and infected removable media like USB drives.

How Does it Work?

  1. The virus attaches itself to a legitimate program or file
  2. When the infected file is executed, the virus becomes active
  3. The virus replicates itself by attaching to other files and programs
  4. It spreads to other systems through file sharing, email attachments, or removable media
  5. The virus may remain dormant until triggered by a specific event or date

Real-World Example

The ILOVEYOU virus, which appeared in 2000, was one of the most destructive viruses in history. It spread via email with the subject line "ILOVEYOU" and an attachment named "LOVE-LETTER-FOR-YOU.TXT.vbs". Once opened, it overwrote various file types and sent copies of itself to everyone in the user's address book, causing an estimated $10 billion in damages worldwide.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Install and regularly update reputable antivirus software
  • Never open email attachments from unknown senders
  • Keep your operating system and software up to date
  • Regularly backup important files to external storage
  • Scan removable media before opening files
  • Be cautious when downloading files from the internet
