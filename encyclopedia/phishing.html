Phishing

Medium Severity

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack that uses email or malicious websites to trick victims into revealing personal information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or other sensitive data. Attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy entities in electronic communications, often mimicking legitimate organizations like banks, social media platforms, or government agencies.

How Does it Work?

  1. Attackers craft convincing emails or messages that appear legitimate
  2. These messages often create urgency or fear to prompt immediate action
  3. Victims are directed to fake websites that mimic real ones
  4. Victims enter sensitive information on these fake sites
  5. Attackers collect the entered information for malicious purposes

Real-World Example

The 2016 Democratic National Committee (DNC) email leak was initiated through a sophisticated phishing campaign. Russian hackers sent spear-phishing emails to DNC staff, tricking them into clicking on malicious links that installed malware on their computers. This led to the theft of thousands of emails and documents, which were later released publicly, impacting the U.S. presidential election.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Verify the sender's identity before responding to requests for information
  • Look for signs of suspicious emails (misspellings, urgent language)
  • Hover over links to see the actual destination before clicking
  • Log into accounts directly through bookmarks, not email links
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible
  • Report suspected phishing attempts to your IT department
