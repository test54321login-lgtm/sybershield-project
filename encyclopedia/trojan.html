Trojan

High Severity

What is a Trojan?

A Trojan horse, or simply Trojan, is a type of malware that disguises itself as legitimate software. Trojans are typically spread through social engineering, such as tricking users into opening email attachments or downloading software from untrustworthy websites. Unlike viruses and worms, Trojans generally do not self-replicate.

How Does it Work?

  1. The Trojan appears as a legitimate program or file to trick the user
  2. User downloads and executes the seemingly harmless file
  3. Once activated, the Trojan performs malicious activities in the background
  4. It may create backdoors, steal data, or install other malware
  5. The Trojan remains hidden while continuing its malicious activities

Real-World Example

ZeuS (also known as Zbot) is one of the most notorious banking Trojans. It was first detected in 2007 and has been responsible for stealing millions of dollars from bank accounts worldwide. ZeuS spreads through phishing emails and drive-by downloads, then monitors web traffic to capture banking credentials and other sensitive information.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Only download software from official and trusted sources
  • Be wary of email attachments, especially from unknown senders
  • Keep your operating system and applications updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software with real-time scanning
  • Enable pop-up blockers to prevent drive-by downloads
  • Verify digital signatures on downloaded software
