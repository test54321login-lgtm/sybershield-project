Spyware

Medium Severity

What is Spyware?

Spyware is malicious software that secretly monitors and collects information about a person's or organization's computing habits. It can capture keystrokes, record passwords, track browsing habits, and collect personal information without the user's knowledge or consent. Spyware often comes bundled with freeware or shareware programs.

How Does it Work?

  1. Spyware is installed on a device without the user's knowledge
  2. It runs silently in the background collecting data
  3. Data collected includes browsing habits, login credentials, and personal info
  4. The information is transmitted to third parties without consent
  5. Spyware may also install additional malware or change system settings

Real-World Example

Superfish was a controversial adware program pre-installed on Lenovo laptops in 2014-2015. It injected ads into web pages and used a man-in-the-middle approach to intercept encrypted connections, weakening SSL security. This created a significant security vulnerability that could expose users to other forms of malware and surveillance.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Use reputable anti-spyware software and keep it updated
  • Be cautious when installing free software from the internet
  • Read installation agreements carefully to avoid bundled software
  • Regularly review installed programs and remove unnecessary ones
  • Keep your operating system and browsers updated
  • Use browser extensions that block tracking and ads
