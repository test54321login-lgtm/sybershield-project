Ransomware

Critical Severity

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software designed to block access to a computer system or data until a sum of money is paid. Most ransomware encrypts files on the victim's device, demanding payment for the decryption key. Ransomware attacks are typically carried out using a trojan disguised as a legitimate file or link.

How Does it Work?

  1. The ransomware enters the system through phishing emails or software vulnerabilities
  2. Once executed, it begins encrypting files on the local drive and network shares
  3. Encryption uses strong algorithms that make files inaccessible without the key
  4. The ransomware displays a ransom note with payment instructions
  5. Victims are told to pay a fee to receive the decryption key

Real-World Example

WannaCry was a global ransomware attack that occurred in May 2017, affecting more than 200,000 computers across 150 countries. It exploited a vulnerability in Microsoft Windows and demanded payments in Bitcoin. The attack affected hospitals, banks, government agencies, and major corporations worldwide, causing billions in damages.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Maintain regular backups of important data on external drives
  • Keep your operating system and software updated
  • Use reputable antivirus and anti-malware software
  • Be cautious with email attachments and links from unknown sources
  • Implement network segmentation to limit spread
  • Train employees to recognize phishing attempts
