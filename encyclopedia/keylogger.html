Keylogger

High Severity

What is a Keylogger?

A keylogger is a type of surveillance software or hardware that records every keystroke made on a computer keyboard. Keyloggers can be used for legitimate purposes like parental control or employee monitoring, but they are often used maliciously to steal passwords, credit card numbers, and other sensitive information. They operate covertly in the background, capturing keystrokes and transmitting them to attackers.

How Does it Work?

  1. The keylogger is installed on the target system, often through malware
  2. It operates in the background, recording all keystrokes
  3. Recorded data is stored locally or transmitted to remote servers
  4. Attackers can retrieve captured login credentials and sensitive data
  5. Some keyloggers also capture screenshots or record mouse movements

Real-World Example

The DarkComet Remote Access Trojan (RAT) included keylogging capabilities among its many malicious features. It was used in various cyberespionage campaigns, including targeted attacks against government institutions and private companies. The malware could capture keystrokes, passwords, and other sensitive information, sending it back to the attacker's command and control server.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Use reputable antivirus and anti-malware software with behavioral analysis
  • Keep your operating system and applications updated
  • Be cautious when downloading and installing software
  • Use on-screen keyboards for entering sensitive information
  • Monitor running processes for suspicious activity
  • Consider using hardware-based security keys for authentication
